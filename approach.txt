  epoch 10-20:  80% teacher, 20% model's own predictions

  epoch 30+:    20% teacher, 80% model

  Why RL doesn't help here

  You have supervision. You have actual human scanpaths — you know exactly where people looked and for how long. When you have
  ground truth trajectories, supervised learning (teacher forcing) is almost always better than RL. RL is for when you don't have
  demonstrations and need to discover behavior from a reward signal.

  Using RL when you have human scanpaths is like using reinforcement learning to teach a model to translate French→English when you
  already have a parallel corpus. You can, but why would you?

  What actually makes the paper strong

  Reviewers will care about:
  1. The visual span / degraded input idea — novel, cognitively motivated
  2. Does it predict human reading measures better? — FFD, gaze, TRT correlations
  3. Does it produce human-like scanpaths? — regression rates, skip rates, saccade length distributions
  4. Ablation: does the sequential mechanism matter? — compare to your current one-pass model

  None of that requires RL. Teacher forcing with scheduled sampling gets you there.

  My recommendation

  Teacher forcing → scheduled sampling → Gumbel-softmax. Pick whichever gives the best results. Mention RL as future work in the
  discussion section — "the architecture could also be trained with policy gradient methods" — and move on. Nobody will fault you
  for it.

  The novelty is the architecture (sequential reading with degraded neural representations), not the training algorithm.
